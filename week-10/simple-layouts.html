Simple Layouts

One of the easiest layouts you can do in web design is take well-structured content, place it in a container and center the container.

Studies show people are most comfortable when reading across a page about 60-70 characters across.

On the web we try not to expand a container beyond 960px since it's still a pretty standard screen size.

A centered layout

You need only two components to create a simple, centered layout:

  1. A div wrapping your content in your HTML file
  2. CSS declarations on that div specifying width and margin

Working with max-width

What if we wanted our centered container's content to adjust as it became smaller? (Maybe someone is viewing the site on a phone or tablet.)

When we use the max-width property, we're telling the browser to not let our content stretch beyond the value specified.

This provides us with flexibility to set space for our content in a design, but also make it elastic if the viewport is smaller than the alotted space.

Working with min-width

Some browsers (Firefox) will crush a block element without mercy.

What if our design called for flexibility, but not at the total expense of our content?

Using min-width will limit how much an element can be re-sized.
